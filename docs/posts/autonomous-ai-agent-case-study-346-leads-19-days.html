The Starting Point — What Existed Before

Before Dev Agent was deployed on February 21, 2026, devops.gheware.com looked like most B2B businesses: technically strong, marketing-weak.

  • Blog content: ~34 posts written manually by the founder over two months (January–February 2026)
  • CRM leads: 0. No structured pipeline, no tracking, no follow-up system
  • Social media: Sporadic, manual posting — whenever time allowed
  • Outreach: Manual and untracked — no sequences, no drips, no scoring
  • SEO: No systematic optimization — content published without keyword strategy

This is the default state for most skilled founders. The business works. The marketing doesn't. Not because they don't know what to do — but because there aren't enough hours to do it consistently while also running the business.

The Daily Autonomous Workload — 13 Cron Jobs

The Dev agent doesn't take weekends. It doesn't context-switch. It doesn't forget follow-ups. Every day, 13 automated workflows execute on schedule:

  • 1 SEO-optimized blog post — researched, written, published, and submitted to Google Indexing API
  • 4 social media posts — across Facebook and Instagram, spanning AI, DevOps, Kubernetes, and Observability content verticals
  • 2 video reels — AI-generated book promo content (launched March 12)
  • Prospecting — Monday through Saturday, new leads identified, scored, and added to CRM
  • Email drip check — every 10 minutes, sequences triggered based on lead status and time in pipeline
  • Weekly SEO optimization — keyword gap analysis, content improvement suggestions, Search Console review
  • Memory consolidation — weekly synthesis of what's working, FEEDBACK.md updated, strategy refined

That's what 13 cron jobs looks like. Not a set of reminders. An actual operating system for marketing.
